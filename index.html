Tracking Eviction Trends for FY ‘24 in Oregon PRESENTATION BY MINA MOHEBBI & DAVID CAMPER

The Eviction Research Network collects, analyzes, & maps eviction data while helping other researchers map & analyze theirs. For our Capstone Project, we were tasked with assisting ERN with completing, interpreting & visualizing the extension of their 2024 Q1 report of evictions in the state of Oregon. Our task was to tell the story of eviction rates in the state as it relates to racial disparities and historical trends of discrimination.

Methodology:

Automated data ingestion & cleaning: Pulled in pre-aggregated eviction records for fiscal year 2024, removed counties with no data, standardized dates to month-start, and put together historical and recent data in one table.

Reusable summary functions: Defined functions that compute things like monthly totals by county, percent changes vs. historical averages, or rolling 12-month summaries, so we can get consistent outputs for any date range.

Demographic level analysis: Eviction counts are joined with American Community Survey (Census) data (race, household headship, income, rent, neighborhood classifications) to calculate eviction rates per 1,000 renters for different subgroups (e.g., by race, by income bracket, by rent quartile).

Rate & ratio calculations: for each group, we derived per-1,000-renters eviction rates, then computed comparative metrics (e.g., Black-to-White rate ratios or percent differences from a baseline year) to highlight disparities.

Automated chart publishing: finally, cleaned and summarized tables were automatically pushed to DataWrapper via its API, to create our visualizations.

Oregon Eviction Trends Pancake Plot

• HB 2001 passed in March 2023, and it made changes to the eviction process by extending the notice period required before an eviction complaint can be filed.

• Our pancake plot shows 2024 as having the highest ever recorded peaks in eviction filings since 2019. It spells a continued demand for continued interventions.

Oregon Eviction Trends Line Plot

• White and other racial group renters follow a lower but parallel trend to the state average of evictions per 1000 renters.

• Evictions for black renters have consistently surpassed all other demographics by large margins, with Latinx renters close behind.

•Black renters also seem to have a contrasting pattern in the increases and decreases in eviction filings when compared to Latinx renters.

Oregon Eviction Trends Neighborhood Plot
(By Median Rent)

• Eviction rates for black renters are consistently higher across all neighborhood types, with the highest rates in urban neighborhoods.

• The middle median rent brackets have higher eviction rates across demographic groups than the lowest and highest quartiles.

• Historically marginalized racial groups consistently face the most evictions, regardless of median rent.

Oregon Eviction Trends Neighborhood Plot
(By Neighborhood Composition)

• Diverse neighborhoods (>10% composition of each racial group) have the highest rate of evictions across the majority of racial groups.

• White evictions are consistently low across all neighborhood compositions except Diverse, in which the evictions for white renters are significantly higher. This could be attributed to the history of white flight or remnants of de facto segregation, leaving diverse neighborhoods starved of higher income jobs and opportunities.


Key Takeaways

• Creating pipelines using existing tools and libraries like Datawrapper, Leaflet, and the neighborhood package help translate data into unique and effective visualizations.

• Black and Latine renters in Oregon were overwhelmingly negatively impacted by evictions in 2024, with indications that rates will continue to rise.

• There is a need for systemic intervention to reduce the impact on vulnerable renting groups.
